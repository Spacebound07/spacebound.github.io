Before you, my life was composed in muted tones; functional, steady, untouched by wonder,
as though I had learned to exist without ever truly feeling the weight of being alive.

And then you happened not abruptly, not violently,
but like dawn unfurling itself across a reluctant sky,
soft at first… and then impossible to ignore.

You did not simply enter my world, you re-authored it.
Every ordinary moment you touched seemed to gather meaning,
as if time itself paused, just slightly, to admire what you had made of it.

There is a beauty in you that defies simplicity;
not something that can be captured in passing glances,
but something that reveals itself in the quiet intricacies of who you are.

In the way your eyes seem to hold entire constellations,
in the way your presence feels like both fire and refuge,
something that warms me without ever asking me to withstand the burn.

Your mind
God, your mind
it moves with a kind of luminous precision,
effortlessly weaving logic with curiosity,
sharp, discerning, endlessly alive.
You don't just understand the world,
you challenge it, reshape it,
leave it slightly better than you found it
and somehow, you've done the same to me.

And your wit
so instinctive, so beautifully timed
it disarms me in ways I never saw coming.
You have this quiet magic of taking the heaviest days
and making them feel… bearable, sometimes even beautiful.

But it is your strength that undoes me the most.
Not loud, not performative
but resolute, unyielding, deeply rooted.
The kind that endures. The kind that continues,
even when no one is watching,
even when it would be easier to stop.
And still, you remain gentle
as if resilience and tenderness have found a way to coexist within you.

You have turned my life into something vivid
a canvas I didn't realize was incomplete
until you filled it with color.
On days that would have otherwise faded into nothing,
you have given meaning, warmth, permanence.

Loving you has not been a moment
it has been a transformation.
A slow, beautiful unraveling of everything I thought I knew about love,
and a quiet reconstruction into something far more profound.

And now, when I think of the future,
I don't see it as something distant or uncertain
I see it as a place where you are.
In the mundane mornings, in the unremarkable afternoons,
in the quiet nights where nothing extraordinary happens
except the simple, miraculous fact that we are still choosing each other.

I want all of it with you
the laughter, the struggles, the becoming.
I want a life that is not perfect, but ours
built slowly, deliberately, with the same care you have given my heart.

Because if loving you has taught me anything, it is this
that forever is not a promise to be feared,
but something to be longed for,
if it means I get to spend it
learning you, loving you,
again and again,
in ways I have yet to discover.
